United's Hidden Award Chart

May 6, 2026 by Worldly Express

United miles have been devalued over and over again, though not at the level of Skymiles yet. The miles are dynamic for their own flights but partners are not. What I found is that they also follow a similar award chart style as Air Canada, a mix of zones and distance bands. However, their's is much more complicated. Today, I will be sharing exactly how many miles you need and where you can go to maximize your United miles. However, this is my first rough attempt so please email me at worldlyexpressmail@gmail.com with any questions, comments or concerns if there is something that isn't explained well.

Europe Exceptions

Time to kick off the first of many complications with United's award chart.

  • Air France, KLM, and Tarom intra Europe economy awards cost 10,000 miles instead of 15,000 miles.
  • For intra Europe zone, flights departing and arriving in the same country would cost double the number of miles. For example, BCN-CDG-MAD would cost 20,000 miles in economy while BCN-CDG-LIS would only cost 10,000 miles.

LATAM

For flights within South America, LATAM awards from United follow a distance based award chart. These awards also price by each segment, meaning that a layover flight would be the sum of the cost for each individual legs, not pricing the total distance traveled.

WestJet

United miles guides often mention the value to be had redeeming miles for WestJet flights. For flights more than 2 months out, flights within North America follow a set price. Flights within Canada and the Continental US cost 12,500 miles in economy while flights to or from Hawaii will cost 22,500 miles. For flights to the rest of North America, they have set pricing depending on the city pairs but to me, it doesn't follow any rhyme or reason.

Because cash prices can get kind of high, redemptions from US to Canada could provide great value with proper planning.

Hawaiian Airlines

Hawaiian Airlines awards cost 7,500 miles for economy flight within the Hawaiian islands. This option might disappear once Hawaiian Airlines fully merge with Alaska Airlines.

Layover Rules

1 Layover

1 layover still prices as a direct flight would. The start and final city are the only considerations. CDG - RUH - DEL will price the same as CDG - DEL.

2 Layovers

2 layover gets a bit tricky. If the operating airline is the same throughout, say from CDG - JED - RUH - DEL all on Saudia, then the award would price the same as direct and 1 layover. However, if the operating carrier changes, then the award would be priced as if there are 2 different awards being booked on each of the separate airlines.

For example, NBO - AMS - JED - BKK, where NBO(Africa) - AMS - JED(Middle East) is on KLM and JED(Middle East) - BKK(Asia) on Saudia Airlines, would cost 40,000 miles from Africa to Middle East and 40,000 miles from Middle East to Asia. The total for the entire award would be 80,000 miles.

However, the same flight but with the layovers in different cities would price different. NBO - JED - AMS - BKK, where NBO (Africa) - JED - AMS (Europe) is on Saudia and AMS (Europe) - BKK (Asia) on KLM, would cost 35,000 miles from Africa to Europe and 50,000 miles from Europe to Asia. The total for the entire award would be 85,000 miles.

As you can see, where the change in operating carriers matters because it would change in the price of the award.

Booking by agent: If layover itinerary is not found online, data points suggest that calling an agent to book may price each segment individually.
