Ultimate Guide to Hilton Hotels

March 27, 2025 by Worldly Express

To me, Hilton is a program that is the most friendly to people like me that don't have many vacation days to travel. For us, Hilton offers unique perks that do not require much time or monetary investments into the program. I am here to walk you through how to get the most out of the Hilton Honors program!




Top 5 Takeaways

  1. Purchasing Points: Hilton points very often go on sale for 0.5 cents per point throughout the year. To make math on how much awards cost and how many points you're earning, divide award prices by 200 for the cash cost and divide credit card multipliers by 2 for a more "accurate" multiplier.
  2. 5th Night Free: The 5th night is free on all award bookings.
  3. Top Tier Elite Status: The Hilton Aspire card can be an easy way to get top tier diamond status for little effort.
  4. Free night certificate: The Hilton Aspire awards one a free night certificate annually, which are available to use for any Hilton property with standard award availability. Both the Amex Hilton Aspire and Hilton Surpass card also have a free night certificate as a spend incentive he Hilton Aspire awards one annually. This could net $1000+ when used for luxurious properties, like Waldorf Astoria, Conrad and even their partner, SLH.
  5. Elite Food Benefit: Elite status "food" benefit is granted to both Gold and Diamond members. Internationally, this is a free breakfast for 2, as you can expect with most hotel programs. However, in the United States this credit is dolled out in "food and beverage credits", which could be used for any meal of the day but usually not enough to cover the entire meal.

Earning Hitlon Honors Points

Hilton Stays

In general, Hilton Honors has made earning from stays very simple. The Hilton points earning rate is based on the amount of money spent, with the Home 2 Suites and Tru Hotels brands earning half of what other is shown below. This is most likely due to the properties focused on the budget market.





General Member: 10 points per dollar
Silver : 12 points per dollar
Gold : 18 points per dollar
Diamond : 20 points per dollar

Please do note that like most other hotel programs, booking through third party like Expedia or travel portals don't earn points. There are also frequent promotions that will triple the amount base points earned, making stays quite lucrative.

Meetings and Events

Earn 1 point per dollar when hosting a meeting or event at a Hilton hotel. The maximum number of points that can be earned is 100,000 points each year.

Dining Portal

Much like other programs, Hilton allows you to link a credit card to their dining program to earn Hilton Honors points on partnered restaurants. Note that linking a credit card to one dining program will usually unlink it from others.

Transfer Partners

All of the below transfer partners represent a poor value at the ratio they are transfer. This is due to the frequent point sales that make me value Hilton points at 0.5 cents per point.

Amex MR: 1 MR:2 Hilton Honors points. This pegs the MR point at 1 cent per point. MR points are worth at a minimum of 1.1 cents per point when cashing out through Charles Schwab Invest with rewards so this is not a good deal. However, Hilton may offer transfer bonuses of 1.3x-1.5x, which would bring the MR point value to 1.3-1.5 cents per point respectively. While not a great deal, it is better than the 1 cpp cash out rate.
Diners Club: 1250 Club Rewards to 2000 Hilton Honors Points
Virgin Atlantic: 10,000 Virgin Points to 15,000 Hilton Honors points
Amtrak: 5000 Amtrak to 10,000 Hilton Honors points

Everyday Partners

Lyft: Earn 3 points per dollar on regular Lyft rides and 2 poitns per dollar on shared Lyft rides. Would not recommend linking your Hilton account as you can earn 2 Bilt points per dollar instead, which are more valuable.
Car Rentals: Earb 500 points per day and 500 points per rental, up to 5000 points for renting with Alamo, Enterprise, or National.

Redeeming Hilton Honors Points

Hilton Stays

Hilton no longer has an award chart but their awards can still be highly lucrative for higher end properties. The awards are categorized into standard and premium rates, where the latter costs significantly more in price for the same room. Standard rates typically price at 0.5 cents per point while premium is much less. Standard awards also have a maximum cap of 150,000 points, which makes some of the partner SLH hotels that cost over $1000 a night to be extremely good value redemptions. The next 3 sections offer some quirks of using your Hilton Honors stays.

Pay with Points and Money

Hilton allows for the flexibility to pay with a combination of points and cash, where the number of points can be reduced by 1,000 point incrememnts. Usually the cost to do so with cash is a hair less than the award CPP so paying in full with points will always represent better value.

Fifth Night Free

For Hilton members that have Silver, Gold, or Diamond status, every award stay with 5 or more consecutive nights will have the fifth night free. The maximum number of nights per stay is 20 nights, so a total of 4 free award nights per stay. Note that pay with points and cash will not trigger this benefit.

Resort Fees

Award stays don't have resort fees imparted. Point cost represents total cost of stay.

Points Pooling

Another way to increase your points is to pool your family and friend's poits together. Members can transfer up to 500,000 points to another member and receive up to 2 milion points each year. Note that to join a pool, the joining member must have had their account for at least 30 days and have at least 1000 points.

Donating Points

Hilton allows for donation of points through a program called points worthy.

Partner Redemption

I'm going to list additional ways that you can redeem Hilton points with their partners but these all represent terrible value. The only time I see value in redeeming points this way is if you earn millions of Hilton points each year and you can't use them all.

Amazon: Pay for Amazon purchases for 0.2 cents per point.
Event Vouchers: Buy $50 event vouchers for meetings and events at Hilton for 0.2 cents per point.
Car Rental: Pay with points at Alamo, Enterprise, or National for 0.2 cents per point.
Lyft: Buy Lyft credit for 0.22 cents per point.
Ticketmaster: Pay Ticketmaster puchrases for 0.2 cents per point.
Transfer Partners: Transfer Hilton points at a rate of 10,000 Hilton points to 1,000-2,600 airline miles. Most airlines fall under the 1,000 range, meaning 1 airline mile per 10 Hilton points.

Hilton Elite Status

Elite Status Requirements

Hilton has 3 tiers of elite status after general members. Each tier could be earned with either enough points, stays, or nights in a year. Just like all major hotel groups, elite nights and points are only earned when booking direct or through portals like Amex FHR. It is my opinion that the easiest way to qualify for Hilton elite status is to hold a credit card.

Elite Status Benefits

As you reach higher Hilton elite status, the rewards get progressively more rewarding.



To me, the sweet spot would be gold, where you can receive the food and beverage credit. Other benefits I value are as below:

  • Fifth night free
  • Free Bottled Water (I know it's odd but I hate paying for water and some hotels don't have water fountains.)
  • Space Available Room Upgrades (Although that is hard to come by)
  • Executive Lounge Access

Lifetime Diamond Status

Hilton has one way to earn lifetime status. By being a diamond member for 10 years and either earning 2 million base points or 1000 paid or award nights, you can become a lifetime diamond member. To earn 2 million base points, this pretty much means spending $200,000 on Hilton hotels (excluding Tru and Home 2 Suites brands). Counting award stays is a pretty friendly action towards those of us in the points and miles game!
