Ultimate Guide to IHG Hotels

March 27, 2025 by Worldly Express

For those that travel less than 5 times a year, IHG is a fantastic program. For casual travelers, IHG offers unique perks that do not require sinking much time or money into the program. I am here to walk you through how to get the most out of the IHG program!




Top 5 Takeaways

  1. Purchasing Points: IHG points very often go on sale for 0.5 cents per point throughout the year.
  2. InterContinental Ambassador Status: Buy InterContinental Ambassador status for $225 or 45,000 IHG points. The status comes handy for those that do not want a credit card or those that often stay at InterContinental Hotels.
  3. 4th Night Free: The 4th night is free on all award bookings. Combined with the points sales and IHG awards often costing 0.5 cents per point or more, buying points for a free fifth night is really easy to achieve.
  4. Top Tier Elite Status: The IHG Aspire card can be an easy way to get top tier diamond status for little effort.
  5. Free night certificate: The IHG credit cards come with or have opportunities to earn a free night certificate, usable at any IHG property with standard award availability. This could net $1000+ when used for luxurious properties, like Waldorf Astoria, Conrad and even their partners, like SLH.

Earning IHG Points

IHG Stays

The IHG points earning rate is based on the amount of money spent, with the budget extended stay brands Staybridge Suites and Candlewood Suites earning half of what other brands earn, as shown below.



Club (no status): 10 points per dollar
Silver : 12 points per dollar
Gold : 14 points per dollar
Platinum : 16 points per dollar
Diamond : 20 points per dollar

Please do note that like most other hotel programs, booking through third party like Expedia or travel portals don't earn points.

Buying Points

My favorite way of earning IHG Points (except credit card welcome offers)! Normally, points are on sale for 1 cent per point but many times throughout the year, points sales come with a 100% bonus , pretty amazing when coupled with 4th night free! Unfortunately, there is maximum of 200,000 points purchased per account per year, a total of 400,000 points with the 100% purchase bonus.

Meetings and Events

Earn 3 point per dollar when hosting a meeting or event at a IHG hotel, up to 60,000 points each year.

Dining Portal

Much like other programs, a credit card can be linked to their dining program to earn IHG points on partnered restaurants. Note that linking a credit card to one dining program will usually unlink it from others. As IHG points are not worth that much, I would advise against this.

Transfer Partners

All of the below transfer partners represent a poor value at the ratio they are transfer. At the transfer ratio listed below, I would highly discourage transferring.

Chase UR: 1 Ultimate Rewards point to 1 IHG points.

Bilt: 1 Bilt point to 1 IHG points.

Diners Club: 1250 Club Rewards to 2000 IHG Points

Virgin Atlantic: 10,000 Virgin Points to 15,000 IHG points

Amtrak: 5000 Amtrak points to 10,000 IHG points

Everyday Partners

OpenTable: Earn 150 points for each reservation made through IHG.

Car Rentals: Earn 500 points per day and 500 points per rental, up to 5000 points for renting with Alamo, Enterprise, or National.

Redeeming IHG Points

IHG Stays

IHG no longer has an award chart, but rather are categorized into standard and premium rates. Standard rates typically price at 0.5 cents per point while premium is much less. Standard awards also have a maximum cap of 250,000 points. The next 3 sections offer some quirks of paying for IHG stays.

Pay with Points and Money

IHG allows for the flexibility to pay with a combination of points and cash, where the number of points can be reduced by 1,000 point increments. Usually poor value, since the cost to do so with cash is a hair less than the award cents per point.

Fifth Night Free

For IHG members that have Silver, Gold, or Diamond status, every award stay with 5 or more consecutive nights will have the fifth night free. The maximum number of nights per stay is 20 nights, so a total of 4 free award nights per stay. Note that pay with points and cash will not trigger this benefit.

No Resort Fees

Award stays don't have resort fees imposed.

Points Pooling

Members can transfer up to 500,000 points to another member and receive up to 2 million points each year. Note that to join a pool, the joining member must have held their account for at least 30 days and at least 1,000 points.

Donating Points

IHG allows for donation of points through a program called points worthy.

Partner Redemptions

Here are all teh additional ways that IHG points can be redeemed with their partners but these all represent terrible value.

Amazon: Pay for Amazon purchases for 0.2 cents per point.
Event Vouchers: Buy $50 event vouchers for meetings and events at IHG for 0.2 cents per point.
Car Rental: Pay with points at Alamo, Enterprise, or National for 0.2 cents per point.
Lyft: Buy Lyft credit for 0.22 cents per point.
Ticketmaster: Pay Ticketmaster purchases for 0.2 cents per point.
Transfer Partners: Transfer IHG points at a rate of 10,000 IHG points to 1,000-2,600 airline miles. Most airlines fall under the 1,000 range, meaning 1 airline mile per 10 IHG points.

IHG Elite Status

Elite Status Requirements

IHG has 3 tiers of elite status after general members. Each tier could be earned with either enough points, stays, or nights in a year. Just like all major hotel groups, elite nights and points are only earned when booking direct or through portals like Amex FHR and Chase the Edit. It is my opinion that the easiest way to qualify for IHG elite status is to hold the applicable credit card.

Elite Status Benefits



Gold is the sweetspot to receive the food and beverage credit. Other benefits I value are as below:

  • Fifth night free
  • Free Bottled Water (I know it's odd but I hate paying for water and some hotels don't have water fountains.)
  • Executive Lounge Access
  • Space Available Room Upgrades

Lifetime Diamond Status

IHG has one way to earn lifetime status. Lifetime diamond status is earned by being a diamond member for 10 years and either earning 2 million base points or 1000 paid/award nights. To earn 2 million base points, spend $200,000 on IHG hotels (excluding Tru and Home 2 Suites brands). Counting award stays is a pretty nice gesture towards those in the points and miles game!

Rollover Elite Nights

Any excess elite nights after reaching a certain status threshold will count towards the elite qualification of next year.

Milestone Rewards

After reaching 40 elite nights, earn 10,000 bonus points. For every 10 elite nights after that, earn another 10,000 bonus points. At 60 elite nights specifically, earn 30,000 bonus points instead. Honestly, pretty lackluster considering the other hotel program milestone rewards can give guaranteed suite upgrade certificates.

Status Match

IHG offers status matches from many other programs. The status match comes in the form of a 90 day trial, where staying a certain number of nights can retain that status for the rest of the year. The threshold is 8 nights for Gold and 14 nights for Diamond.

IHG Credit Cards

IHG lineup of credit cards is perhaps the most overpowered in the game. The Aspire card gives so much outsized value while the Surpass card provides great multipliers for a co-branded card and a worthwhile incentive to spend on the card.

IHG

The no annual fee IHG is a great downgrade option, like every no annual fee co-branded credit card. However, the free card punches above its weight class in several ways. The card comes with automatic IHG Silver status, which unlocks the IHG 5th night free on award stays. Also, the card has a chance to earn a IHG Free Night certificate as an elevated welcome offer a couple times throughout the year. This certificate is applicable to any IHG Hotels worldwide that have a stand room award night. Since these certificates can be used for SLH hotels, which are usually retailing for $1000+, they are extremely valuable and a great incentive to chase with spend.

IHG Surpass

Like all Amex cards these days, it comes with credits to offset the annual fee, but the credits for this card are pretty easy to use. The $50 every quarter could be used when purchasing a IHG gift card online (if it comes back) or at IHG and restaurants located inside IHG hotels. The card also comes with IHG Gold status, the same status the American Express Platinum. Surprising, the card earns 6x (effectively 3%) on groceries, gas, and dining and 3x (effectively 1.5%) everywhere else, making it a decent everyday earner. In addition, the card also incentives spend by granting 1 IHG Free Night Certificate after $15,000 of spend a year.

IHG Aspire

The most hyped up (and for good reason) of all credit cards, THE IHG Aspire. The already comes with decently easy to use coupons that give $600 in value, more than the annual fee. The card grants IHG's top tier status, Diamond status. This makes IHG status the easiest to achieve. The card also grants a free night certificate each year. This card is the king of all cards!

IHG Business

The card once again comes with $60 a quarter at IHG. The card also gives IHG Gold status, like the IHG Surpass. The card's main draw is the 5x (effectively 2.5%) multiplier on everything. However, there is a cap of $100,000 a year and no free night certificate as a spend incentive. Unfortunately, the direction that IHG took is quite puzzling. The existence of a cap will drive away high business spenders and for the average Joe, the lack of a spend incentive may cause them to look else where (like welcome offers) to concentrate their spend on.
